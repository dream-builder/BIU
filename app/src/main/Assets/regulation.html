TEACHING SYSTEM 
The education system of BIU is typically a North American Model in the fields of Business Administration, Computer Science and Information Technology, UK model in the fields of Law and English and Saudi Arabian Islamic Universities and Al-Azhar University model in the Faculty of Islamic Studies & Shari`ah. The University is a study laboratory for the young learners who seek to excel in their chosen fields of specialization.

THE CURRICULUM
While designing the curriculum, the University followed the principles as suggested by the Sixth International Islamic Conference held at Cape Town, South Africa in 1996. The University has designed the curriculum in such a manner that it leads to the shaping of a man/woman so that he/she becomes spiritual, sound, creative, hard-working, liberally-educated, environmentally aware of socio-political and cultural phenomena and thus he/she becomes an integrated human being.

MEDIUM OF INSTRUCTION
English/Arabic is the medium of instruction, which is indispensable to follow the curriculum and the prescribed texts. Courses on English are incorporated into the curriculum.  To raise the student’s proficiency in English, a number of English courses are administered concurrently with regular course work.

FACULTY MEMBERS
Faculty members of BIU are a selected group of teachers, researchers, professionals and practitioners with proven records of excellence in their respective fields. In addition to the services of its full time faculty members, the university has access to a guide network of academics from home and abroad in various fields, who can be commissioned depending on necessity and convenience.

EXCHANGE/LINK PROGRAM AND CREDIT TRANSFER
As the part of exchange program, recently, BIU has accomplished a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with International Islamic University, Malaysia (IIUM) for the greater interests of both teachers and students for their higher studies. Besides, BIU has the plan to develop exchange programs with leading universities in North America, the U.K, Australia, Egypt, Spain and Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. Such programs will facilitate the exchange of faculty members and students. Besides receiving teaching services of overseas faculty members, the students may get themselves admitted for higher education to those universities through credit transfer and get employment opportunities abroad subsequently. BIU has the provision of accepting students from other recognized universities for admission both at graduate and undergraduate levels through credit transfer.

LOGISTIC SUPPORTS FOR THE STUDENTS

  • State-of-the-art Computer Lab
  • Well stocked Library
  • Audio-visual support service
  • Fully air-conditioned classrooms
  • BIU under Wi-Fi zone

 
PLACEMENT FACILITIES

  • Career development counseling
  • Internship program
  • Job placement services
  • Employment opportunities and networking

 
RESEARCH AND PUBLICATIONS
The university has a research centre and bi-annually it publishes a journal namely “Bangladesh Islami University Journal” (ISSN No. 1995-4182) and other periodicals. By this time, five volumes of the journal have been published.

SCHOLARSHIP / FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE
BIU clearly visualizes that quality students blended with quality teaching are prerequisites for excellent education. So it offers a significant number of scholarships (BIU Trust Scholarship, Vice-Chancellor’s Scholarship, University Scholarship & Departmental Scholarship) and tuition fees waivers (full/partial) to attract the meritorious students.

FREE FACILITIES / SERVICES

  1. BIU Journal
  2. Student Counseling
  3. Tutorial Service
  4. Free Internet Service

 
EXTRA CURRICULAR ACTIVITIES
Extracurricular activities of the students are administered by:

  • Debating Club
  • Islamic Studies Club
  • Business Club
  • Law Club
  • Computer Club
  • English Language Club
  • BIU Sports Club
  • BIU Cultural Club

 

ACADEMIC SESSION
Academic year of BIU is divided into 3 (three) semesters: Spring (January-April), Summer (May-August) & Fall (September-December). The students are required to be registered thrice in a year. In addition to course work, the students are required to participate in the seminars, conferences and workshops.
